## 1. Email hardening — the one small DNS tweak worth making

**Status of the two items I'd flagged:**
- ✅ **DMARC** now reads `p=quarantine; rua=mailto:postmaster@smoothlyoperatingnursery.com` — the reporting address is already in place. Nothing to do.
- ⏳ **Root SPF** is `v=spf1 include:spf.migadu.com` — **missing the trailing "all" mechanism**.

**Commentary — why change it:** the ending of an SPF record tells receivers how to treat mail from servers *not* on your list. Right now there's no ending, so the answer is "no opinion" (neutral) — the weakest setting. Migadu's *own* published record is `v=spf1 include:spf.migadu.com **-all**`, so adding `-all` doesn't fight the provider — it matches what Migadu recommends. It got dropped somewhere during entry.

**Why better:** stops spammers/phishers sending "from" `hello@`/`marzena@yourdomain`, protects your domain reputation and deliverability, and strengthens DMARC.

**Is it safe?** Yes. Your only root-domain sender is Migadu (already listed). The website form (Resend) sends from the `send.` subdomain with its *own* SPF, and forwarding to Gmail uses SRS — none of that is affected by tightening the root.

**Downside of `-all`:** if you later add a tool that sends as `@yourdomain` from the root and forget to authorise it, that mail is rejected until you add it. If you'd rather have a safety margin, use `~all` (softfail — marks instead of rejects). Either beats neutral.

**Action [you]:** In Namecheap → Advanced DNS, edit the root SPF TXT record to:
```
v=spf1 include:spf.migadu.com -all
```
(or `~all` if you prefer the cautious option). One record, one edit. Everything else (DMARC, Resend, Migadu) stays as-is.
